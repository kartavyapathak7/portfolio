Frequently Asked Questions

Standard guidelines recommend 0.8g per kg for sedentary adults. However, current sports science research recommends 1.4 to 2.2g per kg of bodyweight for active individuals, gym-goers, and athletes to maintain lean mass and recover from workouts.
Eating higher amounts of protein supports muscle synthesis, increases satiety (fullness), and slightly boosts metabolic rate via the thermic effect of food. Research shows that intake up to 2.2g per kg is completely safe for healthy individuals.
While you can make minor gains, building muscle requires a positive nitrogen balance, which is only possible with adequate protein intake (at least 1.4-1.6g per kg of bodyweight).
